12-Week Program

This program uses linear periodization across three progressive phases and a deload — designed for advanced lifters on GLP-1 to maximize muscle retention in a caloric deficit.

WEEKLY SCHEDULE
MondayUpper Strength — Bench, OHP, Rows, Pulldowns
TuesdayCardio — HIIT or LISS (your choice)
WednesdayPull Hypertrophy — Back, Biceps, Rear Delts
ThursdayCardio — HIIT or LISS (your choice)
FridayPush Hypertrophy — Chest, Shoulders, Triceps
SaturdayLower Strength + Cardio (HIIT or LISS)
SundayWeigh-In + Rest

Foundation phase. Build work capacity, cement movement patterns, adapt connective tissue. Weights should feel moderately challenging — never maximal. Add weight or reps each week on main lifts.
💡 GLP-1: Appetite suppression is strongest in early weeks. Be disciplined about protein — lean mass is most at risk here.

Load goes up, volume comes down. Goal shifts to expressing and increasing strength. Add weight when you hit the top of the rep range for all sets. Rest 2–3 min on compounds.
💡 GLP-1: Increase pre-workout protein to 35–40g as intensity climbs. Recovery demand rises and appetite won't signal this.

Maximum intensity. Rep ranges drop to 3–5 on primary compounds. Accessory volume trimmed for full recovery. Push past previous strength ceilings and set new PRs.
💡 GLP-1: Consider a brief refeed around week 10 — a short higher-calorie window improves performance without derailing fat loss.

Strategic recovery. Drop volume to 40% of peak week. Accumulated fatigue dissipates so fitness adaptations fully express. After the deload, test new 1RMs and start the next block heavier.
💡 GLP-1: Use deload week to recalibrate nutrition — re-assess protein intake and adjust for body composition changes.
PROGRESSIVE OVERLOAD RULES
• Hit the top of the rep range on all sets → add 5 lbs next session
• Cannot complete the bottom of the rep range → keep same weight
• Failed to match last session → reduce 10% and rebuild
• Log every weight, every session — memory is unreliable
