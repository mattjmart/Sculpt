Phase: Accumulation
12-Week Program

This program uses linear periodization across three progressive phases and a deload. Each phase builds on the last. Designed specifically for advanced lifters on GLP-1 to maximize muscle retention while in a caloric deficit.

WEEKLY SCHEDULE
Monday Upper Strength — Bench, OHP, Rows, Lat Pulldowns
Tuesday HIIT Cardio — 20 minutes
Wednesday Lower Strength — Squat, RDL, Hip Thrust, BSS
Thursday LISS Cardio — 30 minutes Zone 2
Friday Push Hypertrophy — Incline DB, Laterals, Flyes
Saturday Pull Hypertrophy + LISS + HIIT
Sunday Full Rest

Foundation phase. Build work capacity, cement movement patterns, and adapt connective tissue to higher training volume. Weights should feel moderately challenging but never maximal. Add a small amount of weight or a rep each week on main lifts.
💡 GLP-1 tip: Appetite suppression is often strongest in early weeks. Be disciplined about protein targets even when not hungry — this phase is where lean mass is most at risk.
PHASE 2IntensificationWeeks 5–8

Load goes up, volume comes down slightly. The goal shifts to expressing and increasing strength. Rep ranges tighten and weights should feel heavy by the last set. Add weight when you hit the top of the rep range for all sets. Rest 2–3 min on compounds.
💡 GLP-1 tip: Increase pre-workout protein to 35–40g as intensity climbs. Recovery demand rises and appetite will not signal this reliably.
PHASE 3PeakWeeks 9–11

RPE

Maximum intensity. Rep ranges drop to 3–5 on primary compounds. Accessory volume is trimmed to allow full recovery. Goal is to push past previous strength ceilings and set new PRs. Sleep and nutrition are non-negotiable here.
💡 GLP-1 tip: Consider a brief refeed around week 10 — a short higher-calorie window improves performance and signals muscle retention without derailing fat loss.
PHASE 4DeloadWeek 12
Sets
2–3
Reps
8–10
RPE
5–6
Strategic recovery. Drop volume to 40% of peak week, keep weights moderate, and move well. Accumulated fatigue dissipates so fitness adaptations can fully express. After the deload, test your new 1RMs and start the next 12-week block heavier.
💡 GLP-1 tip: Use deload week to recalibrate nutrition. Re-assess protein intake, adjust for body composition changes, and plan your next block.
PROGRESSIVE OVERLOAD RULES
• Hit the top of the rep range on all sets → add 5 lbs next session
• Cannot complete the bottom of the rep range → keep same weight
• Failed to match last session → reduce 10% and rebuild
• Log every weight, every session — memory is unreliable
